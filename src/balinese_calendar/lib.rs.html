4// balinese-date — native Rust implementation of the Balinese Saka Calendar.
5//
6// The first Rust crate for Kalender Bali. Covers:
7//   • Pawukon (210-day cycle): 30 Wuku × 7 days
8//   • Pawewaran: all 10 concurrent week cycles (Eka through Dasa Wara)
9//   • Sasih: lunar months, Penanggal/Pangelong/Purnama/Tilem, Ngunaratri
10//   • Nampih Sasih: intercalary month detection (≥ 2003 / Saka 1925)
11//   • Saka year calculation
12//   • Paringkelan: Jejepan, Ingkel, Watek, Lintang, PancaSuda, Pararasan, Rakam
13//   • Rahinan: holy day detection (Galungan, Kuningan, Saraswati, etc.)
14//   • FlatRecord: columnar data serialization for Arrow, Parquet, and other formats
15//
16// Algorithm basis:
17//   - Ardhana, I.B.S. (2005). "Pokok-Pokok Wariga". Surabaya: Paramita.
18//   - babadbali.com (Yayasan Bali Galang) wewaran & paringkelan algorithms.
19//   - Pendit, N.S. (2001). "Nyepi: kebangkitan, toleransi, dan kerukunan."
20//   - peradnya/balinese-date-js-lib & java-lib (Apache-2.0) — behaviour reference.
21//   - Wikipedia: Balinese Saka Calendar (intercalary month rules)
22//
23// Validation target: kalenderbali.org (I Wayan Nuarsa, Universitas Udayana)
24//
25// ⚠ PRODUCTION NOTE (Nampih Sasih):
26//   Intercalary month placement is computed algorithmically from a 19-year
27//   Metonic-like cycle (saka_year % 19), matching the peradnya reference
28//   implementation. The algorithmic estimate may differ from the PHDI
29//   (Parisada Hindu Dharma Indonesia) declaration by 1 sasih in rare years.
